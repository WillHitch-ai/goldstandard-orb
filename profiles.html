Rule 1
London tight — compression into NY
Highest confidence

London barely moves. Price stays within or just inside the Asian range — no sweep, no trend, no narrative. The session closes without extending meaningfully in either direction. This is the cleanest NY ORB setup: all of the potential energy has been saved for New York's distribution move.

Typical profile shape
Position size Full
Score threshold 4+ standard
TP2 target Fixed 2.5R
AMD phase Distribution
Bias direction Follow ORB breakout
  • London range stays flat — price does not extend beyond Asian high or Asian low
  • No sweep, no trend, no identifiable narrative — pure compression
  • NY creates the entire directional move from zero — this is the cleanest setup
  • No named level required for TP2 — fixed 2.5R is the target
Rule 2
London sweep + reversal — AMD narrative complete
High confidence

London spikes through the Asian high or low — taking liquidity sitting above or below the range — then reverses sharply in the opposite direction. This is the AMD cycle working perfectly: Asia accumulates, London manipulates (the sweep), and NY distributes in the reversal direction. The narrative is clear and complete before the OR forms.

Sweep + reversal shape
Position size Full
Score threshold 4+ standard
TP2 target PDH / PDL or 2.5R
AMD phase Sweep + distribution
Bias direction Reversal direction
  • London spikes clearly through the Asian high or low — takes liquidity — then reverses with conviction
  • The reversal must be sharp. A soft drift back is not a reversal. You need two or more consecutive candles in the new direction
  • Use PDH or PDL as your TP2 target — the sweep creates a clear narrative toward the opposite daily extreme
  • Direction is the reversal — if London swept the Asian high, you are looking short for NY, not long
Rule 3A
London trends to named level — stalls at target
Reduced confidence

London trends consistently in one direction and stalls visibly at a named level — typically PDH, PDL, or a prior day's significant structure. The move has purpose, but it has also consumed a lot of the available directional energy. NY may continue or reverse. Confidence is lower — reduce size and require a higher score.

Trend → stall at named level
Position size Half
Score threshold 6+ elevated
TP2 target Named level
AMD phase Partial distribution
Bias direction Away from stall level
  • London must have trended clearly — not drifted. Four or more candles in the same direction to a visible structure level
  • The stall must be visible: price approaches the named level and slows or reverses before London closes
  • Minimum score is 6. Do not take a 4 or 5 on a Rule 3A day
  • Half position only — London has consumed energy; NY may reverse or chop
Rule 3B
London drifts — no target reached
Low confidence — consider skip

London moved in a direction but reached no obvious named level and created no clear AMD narrative. The session drifted — partially up, partially down, or in one direction without a clean story. NY may do anything. This profile requires the highest score threshold and a clear liquidity target in the breakout direction before you consider trading.

Drift — no target, no narrative
Position size Half or skip
Score threshold 7+ only
TP2 target Next key level (must exist)
AMD phase Unclear
Default decision Skip unless 7+ AND clear target
  • London moved but the direction has no obvious destination — no level was swept, hit, or stalled at
  • Score must be 7 or above before considering a trade — the highest threshold of any profile
  • You must identify a clear liquidity target ahead of TP2. If no target exists, this is a skip regardless of score
  • Half position only. The narrative is weak and the setup is speculative relative to Rules 1 and 2
Rule 4
London whipsaws — no readable narrative
No trade

Multiple reversals during London with no consistent direction. Up, then down, then up again. No readable narrative. No AMD cycle visible. Institutions are not distributing — they are confused, hedging, or the day is structurally noisy. Do not trade this session under any circumstances, regardless of how clean the OR looks.

Whipsaw — multiple reversals
Position size NO TRADE
Score threshold Irrelevant

Action Close charts. Log. Done.
  • No trade. This is absolute. No exceptions for "clean OR" or "high ATR" or strong A+ score
  • Do not watch the session after the OR forms. Close your charts. The temptation to enter grows the longer you watch
  • Correctly identifying and skipping a Rule 4 day is as valuable as taking a Rule 1 winner
  • Recognition: if you cannot identify a single clear London direction within 30 seconds, you are looking at Rule 4
